# Module 1 — Learning Objectives

> **Source:** Module 1 manifest §3 (APPROVED). Mapped here to the approved unit sequence (D-008).
> Objectives are the contract lessons must satisfy; they are not lessons themselves.

## Outcomes

On successful completion of Module 1, a student can:

| ID | Objective | Primary units |
|---|---|---|
| **LO1** | Represent physical quantities (position, displacement, velocity, force) as vectors and reason about units, dimensions, and measurement uncertainty. | U1, U2 |
| **LO2** | Distinguish points, vectors, and frames, and explain why a position is only meaningful relative to a reference frame. | U2, U3 |
| **LO3** | Define and convert between coordinate systems (Cartesian, polar, cylindrical) and identify where each is natural in a robotic context. | U3 |
| **LO4** | Interpret a matrix as a geometric transformation (rotation, scaling, shear, projection) rather than only a grid of numbers. | U4 |
| **LO5** | Apply core linear-algebra operations — matrix–vector products, composition, inverse, transpose, dot/cross products — to robotic problems. | U4, U5 |
| **LO6** | Use trigonometry to relate angles, lengths, and positions in 2D/3D motion and simple perception geometry. | U6 |
| **LO7** | Build simple mathematical models of physical systems and reason about their assumptions and limits. | U7 |
| **LO8** | Implement and visualize the above in Python (NumPy, Matplotlib), producing reproducible notebooks. | U8 |
| **LO9** | Integrate these skills to model the workspace of the Greenhouse Harvesting Robot. | U9 |

## Bloom-level intent (informational)

Objectives target progressively higher cognitive demand across the module: **represent / recall** (LO1–LO3) → **apply / analyze** (LO4–LO7) → **implement / create** (LO8–LO9). This mirrors the curriculum's physical-intuition-to-integration philosophy.

## Exit linkage

Meeting LO1–LO9 is what satisfies the manifest §16 exit criteria — the competency bar for entering Module 2 (SE(3)).
