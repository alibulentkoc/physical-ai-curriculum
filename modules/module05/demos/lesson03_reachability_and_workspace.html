Grab the target; toggle the joint limit. Distance alone isn't enough: with θ₁ limited to [0°, 90°], only part of the ring (shaded) is reachable — a point like (−0.3, 0) drops out even though its distance is fine.
Reachability has two parts: the target must sit in the annulus (distance), and a solution must respect the joint limits. Limits carve the workspace down to the configurations the arm can physically strike.
