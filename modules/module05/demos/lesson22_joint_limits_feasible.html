Grab the target; set the θ₂ range. Elbow-up uses θ₂<0, elbow-down uses θ₂>0. A range keeps only the solutions whose θ₂ lands inside it — squeeze it to exclude both and the target becomes infeasible.
Joint limits filter the math solutions down to the ones the hardware can actually strike. Distance reachability isn't enough — the joints must also be in range.
