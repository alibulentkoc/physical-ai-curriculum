Tilt the middle gimbal (pitch). The map ω = B(φ)·φ̇ relates Euler-angle rates to true angular velocity. As pitch → ±90°, two gimbal axes align, |det B| → 0, and one rotation direction becomes unreachable — gimbal lock.
This is a representation singularity — a flaw of the Euler parameterization, not the robot's geometry. The body can still spin freely; the angle coordinates just can't describe it there.
