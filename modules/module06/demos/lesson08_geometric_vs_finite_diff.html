Pick a joint, shrink Δ. The geometric column (teal) is the exact z×r; the finite-difference estimate (red dashed) perturbs θ by Δ and measures Δp/Δ. As Δ → 0 they coincide — validating the formula.
Finite differences are how you sanity-check a hand-derived Jacobian in code: perturb one joint, divide by the step. Agreement to small Δ is strong evidence the analytic J is right.
