Compare "snap" and "ease". Snap jumps to full speed and stops dead — the acceleration spikes at the ends (huge jerk). Ease rises and falls smoothly, keeping acceleration bounded. Watch the dot and the profiles.
Both moves cover the same distance in the same time. The difference is the shape: snap's instant starts/stops shake the structure and wear motors; ease spreads the effort, so the hardware lasts and the fruit isn't bruised.
