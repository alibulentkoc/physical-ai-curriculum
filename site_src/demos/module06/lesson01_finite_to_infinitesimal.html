Grab the point; shrink the angle θ. The exact rotation moves it along an arc (red chord); the infinitesimal model moves it along the tangent θ×r (teal). As θ → 0 they merge — that's why differential rotation is a vector.
A finite rotation is curved and order-dependent; the first-order piece θ×r is straight and additive. Velocity kinematics lives entirely in that linear approximation.
