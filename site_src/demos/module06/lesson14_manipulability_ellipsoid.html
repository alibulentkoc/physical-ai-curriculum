Pose the arm and watch the ellipse. Its long axis is the direction the tip moves easily (large σ₁); the short axis is the stiff direction (small σ₂). A fat round ellipse = dexterous; a thin one = near singular.
The ellipse is the image of a unit ball of joint velocities under J. Its principal axes are the singular values along the left singular vectors — the geometry of the robot's instantaneous capability.
