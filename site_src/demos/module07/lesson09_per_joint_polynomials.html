Each joint follows a smooth quintic from start to goal. In joint space the move is a straight line; in Cartesian space the tip traces a curve (yellow). Simple per-joint timing, curved tool path.
Point-to-point joint moves are the simplest trajectory: one polynomial per joint, no Cartesian solving. The price is that the tool sweeps a curve — fine for free-space reaches, wrong for straight-line tasks (Unit 4).
