Pick the pixel (xₙ), then slide depth Z. Back-projection sets P_c = Z·(xₙ, yₙ, 1): the point slides outward along the fixed ray as Z grows.
The pixel fixes the ray direction (xₙ). Depth says how far along it the fruit sits. Both X and Y grow in proportion to Z — that's why a far point and a near point on the same ray share a pixel.
