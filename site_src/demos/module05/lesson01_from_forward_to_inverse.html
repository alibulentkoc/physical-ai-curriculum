Grab the target and move it, click to drop. Inverse kinematics asks: what θ reaches it? A one-joint arm can only land on its radius-0.5 circle — on the circle there's a θ, off it there's none.
Forward kinematics goes θ → position. Inverse runs it backward: θ = atan2(y, x) — but only when the target's distance equals the link length. The reachable set is a curve, not an area.
