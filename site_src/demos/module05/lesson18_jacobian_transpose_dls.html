Straighten the arm toward the singularity (θ₂ → 0); raise damping λ. The pseudoinverse step blows up near full extension; the damped step stays moderate. λ trades accuracy for stability.

At λ = 0, DLS equals the pseudoinverse (Newton) and spikes too. Large λ behaves like the Jacobian-transpose: gentle, always stable, but slower and slightly off-target.
