Drive the joints. The Jacobian's two columns are the gripper-velocity directions: column 1 = how the tip moves per unit θ₁, column 2 = per unit θ₂. A small Δθ moves the gripper by J·Δθ.
J packages forward kinematics' local sensitivity. Inverse kinematics runs it the other way: given a desired gripper move, solve J·Δθ = Δp for the joint change.
