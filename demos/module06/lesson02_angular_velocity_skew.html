Grab the point r; set ω. The velocity of a rotating point is v = ω × r = S(ω)·r — perpendicular to r, scaling with distance. The skew matrix S(ω) turns the cross product into a matrix multiply.
In 2D, S(ω) = [[0, −ω], [ω, 0]], so v = (−ω·r_y, ω·r_x). Every point sweeps a circle; the field is rigid rotation about the origin.
