Straighten the arm. The condition number κ = σ₁/σ₂ measures how lopsided the ellipse is. A round ellipse (κ≈1) is well-behaved; a thin one (κ large) amplifies any error in the desired velocity by up to κ when you invert.
κ = 1 is a perfect sphere of motion. As κ → ∞ the arm nears a singularity: solving J·θ̇ = ẋ becomes numerically fragile, tiny ẋ errors exploding into huge joint rates.
