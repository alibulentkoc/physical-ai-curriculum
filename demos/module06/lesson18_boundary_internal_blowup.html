Straighten the arm toward θ₂ = 0. To keep a fixed outward tip speed, the joints must spin faster and faster — joint rates blow up as the arm reaches its boundary singularity (full extension).
θ₂ = 0 (extended) and θ₂ = 180° (folded) are boundary singularities at the workspace edge. Internal singularities sit inside the workspace — they appear in arms with offsets or wrists, not a simple 2R.
