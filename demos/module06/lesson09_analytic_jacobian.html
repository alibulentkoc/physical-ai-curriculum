Pose the arm. The analytic Jacobian differentiates forward kinematics: ∂(x, y, φ)/∂(θ₁, θ₂). The top two rows are the position Jacobian; the orientation row is just [1, 1] — each joint adds to φ.
For a planar arm, orientation is φ = θ₁+θ₂, so its gradient is constant [1, 1]. The position rows vary with pose; together they form the analytic Jacobian of FK.
