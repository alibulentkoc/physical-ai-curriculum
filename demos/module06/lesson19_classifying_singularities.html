Straighten the planar arm. A 2R arm has one singularity type — the elbow (fully extended or folded). Spatial arms add two more: shoulder and wrist. The active type highlights below.
Elbow — arm fully extended (θ₂=0) or folded (θ₂=180°). The tip loses radial motion.
Shoulder (spatial) — wrist centre crosses the shoulder axis; the base joint can't help.
Wrist (spatial) — two wrist axes align; one orientation DOF is lost.
All three are rank-deficiency of J, but each comes from a different joint group. Recognising which guides the fix — reroute, re-grasp, or damp.
