Pose the arm. The velocity ellipse (easy directions to move) and the force ellipse (easy directions to push, τ = JᵀF) are duals: long where the other is short. Speed and strength trade off, perpendicular to each other.
Where the arm moves fastest it pushes weakest, and vice versa. A robot pressing a tool into a surface wants the force ellipse's long axis aligned with the push — often near a singularity.
