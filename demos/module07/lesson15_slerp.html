SLERP vs naive LERP. SLERP rotates along the arc at constant rate, staying unit-length. Linear blend of the two orientations cuts the chord — it shrinks below unit length and turns unevenly.
For unit quaternions SLERP is the only interpolation that's both shortest-arc and constant-rate. The shrinking LERP vector would, after re-normalizing, still rotate unevenly — fast in the middle, slow at the ends.
