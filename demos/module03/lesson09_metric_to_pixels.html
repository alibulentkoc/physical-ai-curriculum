Slide the lens length and the pixel size. The focal length in pixels is f_x = f (mm) ÷ pixel size (mm). Smaller pixels pack more in, so f_x grows.
A lens is built in millimetres; an image is counted in pixels. Dividing the metric focal length by the pixel pitch converts one to the other: f_x = f/sₓ. Halve the pixel size and f_x doubles.
