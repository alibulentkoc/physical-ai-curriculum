Click the observed (distorted) point to grab it, move, click to drop. Undistortion inverts the lens: it recovers the true point. Near the centre nothing moves; at the edges the correction is large.
Undistortion solves "what ideal point would distort to this?" by iterating a few times. The centre is a fixed point of the lens, so it never moves; edge points get pushed back to where the straight world really is.
