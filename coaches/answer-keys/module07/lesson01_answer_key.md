# Answer Key 1.1 — Why Smooth, Safe, Efficient Motion Matters

*Coaches' key. Multiple choice followed by model short answers and grading notes.*

---

**Q1 — B.** Reaching the pose says nothing about the journey; quality is about smoothness, safety, efficiency along the way.

**Q2 — B.** The three goals are smooth, safe, efficient.

**Q3 — B.** Jerky motion bruises fruit (abrupt contact/stop) and wears the mechanism (shock loads).

**Q4 — B.** Smoothness constrains how speed changes over time, not which route is taken.

**Q5 — B.** The velocity layer executes any commanded twist faithfully; it does not smooth bad commands — that is Module 7.

---

**Q6 — model answer.** Carrying coffee, you can lurch and spill or ease in and out and keep it steady — same start, same end, same route across the floor. The difference is entirely the timing of speed (how fast you accelerate and decelerate). A robot's payload and gears similarly feel the changes in motion, not the endpoint, so the same destination admits gentle or violent motions.
*Grading: award credit for the core idea above; accept equivalent phrasings and valid examples.*

**Q7 — model answer.** Smooth — prevents bruised fruit and drivetrain shock (jolts). Safe — prevents collisions with stems/canopy and loss of control near singularities. Efficient — prevents wasted time, distance, and strain. All three together define a good harvest motion.
*Grading: award credit for the core idea above; accept equivalent phrasings and valid examples.*

**Q8 — model answer.** Smoothness (and feasibility). Snapping belts indicates repeated shock loads from abrupt acceleration/jerk — a smoothness failure that the correct final pose hides. The fix lives in the motion command's timing, not in the target.
*Grading: award credit for the core idea above; accept equivalent phrasings and valid examples.*

---

### Common misconceptions to watch for
- Students equating 'reached the pose' with 'moved well' — stress these are separate requirements.
- Students thinking smoothness means slowness — smoothness is about how speed changes, not how fast.
