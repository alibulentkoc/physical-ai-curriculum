# Answer Key — Lesson 2.3: Elbow-Up and Elbow-Down: the Two Solutions

**Coaches only.** Formative.

1. **Why two solutions** — θ₂ = ±arccos(...); the elbow can bend either way.
2. **Both reach the same target** — True.
3. **At the outer boundary the two...** — merge into one straight-arm solution.
4. **Why each elbow sign needs its own θ₁ (short).** The base-corner term β = atan2(L₂ sin θ₂, L₁ + L₂ cos θ₂) depends on θ₂; flipping the elbow sign flips sin θ₂, changing β and therefore θ₁.

**Challenge rubric.** Full credit: equal θ₁ for both solutions would require equal β, hence sin θ₂ = 0, i.e. θ₂ ∈ {0, 180°} — which only happens on the outer/inner boundary, so no interior target shares θ₁. Partial: asserts "only at the boundary" without the sin θ₂ = 0 argument.
