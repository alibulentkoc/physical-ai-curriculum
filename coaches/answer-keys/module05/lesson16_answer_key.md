# Answer Key — Lesson 4.4: From Geometry to Numerical IK (Unit 4 Recap · Midpoint)

**Coaches only.** Formative.

1. **The two halves** — analytical (closed form) and numerical (iteration), bridged by the Jacobian.
2. **Midpoint-ready: solve 2-link closed form, both solutions, FK-verify** — True.
3. **Match** — Analytical → exact, all solutions; Numerical → one solution near a seed, any arm.
4. **What the Jacobian provides (short).** The local linear map from a small joint change to the gripper change, used to turn the gripper error into a joint correction.

**Midpoint checkpoint (coach guidance).** The full readiness assessment is `assessments/module05_midpoint_assessment.md`. A student is ready for Unit 5 if they can (a) state the inverse problem, reachability, and 0/1/many; (b) solve the planar 2-link arm in closed form — both solutions — and verify by FK; (c) explain why a general arm needs numerical methods and what the FK Jacobian provides. If shaky: revisit Unit 1 (problem/reachability), 3.1 (closed form), or 4.2–4.3 (Jacobian + loop).
