# Answer Key — Lesson 8.2

**MC1:** A — "self-improving" means the twin-in-the-loop improved the real outcome (not learned).
**MC2:** A — it pre-validates, foresees the doomed pick, and adapts around it.
**MC3:** A — the capstone runs the full spine: Mirror, Simulate, Monitor, Predict, Adapt.
**MC4:** A — the improvement depends on a faithful enough twin (calibration matters).
**TF5:** True — with a faithful twin, twin-in-the-loop never scores worse than blind, and can score better.

**Short 6 — model answer.** As long as the twin is faithful enough to correctly foresee the relevant outcome — i.e. the sim-to-real gap is small on the effect that matters — the loop's pre-validated choice is at least as good as the blind plan, because the loop only departs from blind when its forecast says doing so is no worse. *Grading:* full credit for tying the guarantee to twin fidelity on the relevant effect.
