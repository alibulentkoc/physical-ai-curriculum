# Answer Key — Lesson 4.1

**MC1:** B — same logic on different worlds (reality has unmodeled effects).
**MC2:** B — unmodeled effects in reality the twin doesn't include.
**MC3:** B — twin predicts harvested / reality skips that fruit.
**MC4:** B — inevitable (a model is simpler than the world) and informative.
**TF5:** True — a divergence is a diagnostic signal, not necessarily breakage.

**Short answer 6 — model response.**
No. The twin ran the identical harvester; the divergence came from an unmodeled effect in reality (the obstacle) absent from the twin's world. A model simpler than reality will miss such effects — that's a model, not a defect. The correct reading: the disagreement points at an effect the twin doesn't model. Expecting a zero gap is the real error.

*Grading notes (3 pts): (1) not broken — identical logic; (2) cause = unmodeled effect / different worlds; (3) gap is inevitable + informative. Full credit needs "not broken" plus the unmodeled-effect cause.*
