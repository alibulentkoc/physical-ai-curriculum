# Answer Key 6.3 — Failure Analysis: What / Where / Who Owns the Fix

*Coaches' key. Multiple choice followed by model short answers and grading notes.*

---

**Q1 — B.** What failed? → Where? → Who owns the fix? — before how to recover.

**Q2 — B.** UNREACHABLE surfaces at the IK seam but is owned by Understand (re-select a reachable target).

**Q3 — B.** Perceive would loop — re-perceiving re-creates the same blocked goal; the owner is Plan.

**Q4 — B.** A fix aimed at the wrong stage wastes time or loops; targeting needs a localised, owned fault.

**Q5 — False.** The owner is who must act, which can differ from where the fault surfaced.

---

**Q6 — model answer.** What failed? No valid path to the goal — the planner could not validate a trajectory (e.g. an obstacle blocks every route). Where? Plan. Who owns the fix? Plan — replan with relaxed timing/path, or escalate to Recover if no path exists. Wrong owner to avoid: Perceive — re-perceiving re-detects the same fruit and re-creates the same blocked goal, looping forever.
*Grading: require Plan as owner and the Perceive-loops counterexample.*
