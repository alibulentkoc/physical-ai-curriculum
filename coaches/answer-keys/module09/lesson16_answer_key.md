# Answer Key 4.4 — Unit 4 Recap and Midpoint Checkpoint: The Forward Path, Assembled

*Coaches' key. Multiple choice followed by model short answers and grading notes.*

---

**Q1 — B.** Perceive → Understand → Plan → Execute (the forward half).

**Q2 — B.** No failure detection or recovery — the midpoint proves composition when nothing goes wrong.

**Q3 — B.** The conjunction of every stage's check, ending in FK of the executed state reaching the target.

**Q4 — B.** Judging tracking, detecting failures, recovering, and orchestrating (Units 5–8).

**Q5 — False.** The forward path works only when nothing goes wrong; detection and recovery are still missing.

---

**Q6 — model answer.** The forward path works only when nothing goes wrong — the run has no failure detection and no recovery, so it cannot yet notice an occluded fruit mid-reach, a diverging tracking error, or a target chosen unreachable under noise, let alone respond; supplying exactly that is what Units 5–8 add. Stating crisply what the midpoint does not cover is its real outcome.
*Grading: credit "works only when nothing goes wrong" plus at least one concrete missing failure case and the Units 5–8 pointer.*
